FAN/WATER COOLING

When it comes to cooling down a computer there are 2 different ways of doing this and that is water/liquid cooled or air cooled.
There really isn't a right or wrong way to cool down a processor but there is in fact a recommended way of cooling down your computer.
If you intend to overclock your processor and graphics card, pushing them to their max limits it is strongly recommended that you
consider liguid/water cooling as this provides a more effective way of not melting things. If you don't intend or don't have a processor
that is capable of overclocking, air cooled is usually sufficient enough to not melt the different components within your computer. One
thing to remember when air cooling a computer is you want cold air to come in and hot air to escape, which means your fans need to be
directed in a specific direction and it is very easy to get the direction of "air flow" confused which can lead to premature failure of parts
