RANDOM ACCESS MEMORY (RAM)

Random access memory is the temporary storage that the cpu often uses to load data instead of contantly searching for it on the hdd/ssd.
Ram is what is considered as "short-term" memory in that ram gives applications a place to store and access data on a short-term basis.
Everytime you power down your computer, your ram is cleared out where as on a hdd/sdd when powered down the information stays on the drive.
If you intend to use a lot of software/applications at one time or applications that require signicant amounts of ram, it's best to try to
as much and as fast ram as your motherboard can sustain. If your intentions are single task oriented such as just gaming or surfing the web
from time to time, massive amounts of ram and speed is not as crucial and your money might be spend better else where (at least if you intend)
