DIGITAL VIDEO/VERSATILE DISC (DVD)

With the advancement of technology in computers, comes advancements in other areas of life such as data storage.
In this section we will not cover hard drives since it has its own section. For many years, computers used to use
what many refer to as a floppy disk 3.5 or 5.25 inch formats. Because computers back in the 70's, 80's, and early 90's
didn't have an operating system nor a hard drive to help control and load software, software and games were loaded onto a floppy disk
and so you would load the disk into a drive where the process of magnetic encoding would take place (read or write information)
on the disk. Fast forward a few years and enters into the market CD's and DVD's. I'm sure many of us know what a cd/dvd is
but for those who don't know a CD-ROM (compact disc-read only memory) or DVD-ROM(digital video disc-read only memory)
is another format of storage kind of like smaller lp record or a laserdisc(even bigger dvd!). The ROM part in the name refers
to player that is able to read the disk using beams of light to read the information on the disc. We still use these formats
but are used mainly for entertainment such as movies and are less practical for "storing data". In todays society, it's
practical to use either what is known as a portable external hard drive or a usb thumbdrive since since the amount of storage
