HARD DISK DRIVE/SOLID STATE DRIVE (HDD/SDD)

The hard drive commonly referred to as storage and is exactly what it means in that it stores information. There are many different
ways of storing information but for the sake of boredom we will just stick with common storage found internally inside a computer.
The more common types of storage that are found in todays computers are hard drive disk (HDD) and/or solid state drive. There
are some key differences as well as some postives and negatives to both. Hdd generally hold more storage, but are considerably
slower than ssd to read the information on the drive itself due to the fact that it is mechanical and has moving parts whereas an ssd does
not. Think of a record player and the arm that swings over to the record that plays music. Well, imagine 5-10 records stacked on top of
each other spinning extremely fast and the arms constantly and consistently moving back and forth searching for data written on the disk. That is
what is meant by mechanical. An ssd consists of integrated ciruits and microchips. Even though ssd are extremly fast with data transfer, one
drawback is that there are no warning signs of when an ssd might fail and the information considered to be lost forever. With hdd you can often
detect when it might begin to start failing and more often than not the data/information can be retrieved. There is no right or wrong kind of
of storage you use in your computer but you do need to ask yourself questions: do I want to access my information quickly, if the drive should fail
do I want the information recovered, how much storage do I really need, etc. When building a gaming computer you can use either/or hdd/ssd but
