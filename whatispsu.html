POWER SUPPLY UNIT (PSU)

The Power Supply Unit or PSU for short is what provides the power from the outlet in your wall to your computer. Inside your
computer the psu directly powers the mobo, the cpu, cd/dvd-rom, hdd/sdd and sometimes the fans/watercooling. PSUs come in
different wattage ranges. Your basic computer in today's world often uses between 300w-500w depending on certain aspects of
computer such as type of processor, fans/watercooling (how man fans), dedicated gpu, how many hdd/ssd are in use, etc. Depending
on how you build your computer, many gaming computers require a minimum of 500w of power up to somestimes 1400w. If you are lost
or unsure of how much wattage you might need, there are several online resources that provide a wattage calculator assuming you know
the parts you intend to use and it will calculate how much wattage is needed. For me, when I'm building a custom computer I like to
about 100w more than what is recommended since there might be the potential for future upgrades. Just seems silly to me that if you
use the exact amount of wattage and you want to upgrade sometime in the future you will have to upgrade your psu as well. Why not spend
an extra $5-$10 now as opposed to an additional $70-$150 further down the road? Click on the picture to return to the parts page. :D
