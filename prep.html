Shopping and Preparation

Before we begin to start building our PC, shopping for the components/parts and be a little bit of a hastle and often times
very intimidating but fear not for I will give you some great pointers and guide you through the process. Now when it comes to
gaming pc parts, like many products on the market today there are certain brands that are consider high-tier, mid-tier, and bottom-tier
brands. For instance (and for the record this is just my personal opinion), a few high-tier brands when it comes to gaming pc parts
include brands such as Nvidia, EVGA, and ASUS. Mid-tier brands include brands such as MSI, Gigabyte, and ASRock and bottom-tier brands
include Intel, Acer, Dell, and Biostar. Now I will say some brands excel at certain parts even though they may be consider a mid-tier or
bottom-tier. An example is MSI and their parts. MSI makes a breat motherboard but their graphics cards are often very flimsy and fragile.
Another thing to consider when building a gaming pc is a budget. More often than not, your top-tier parts will be anywhere from about 10-25%
more expensive and may perform exactly the same as a mid-tier brand. The last thing you need to consider is how do you know if all of these
parts will fit and/or compatible with each other. Well you are in luck because there are websites such as https://pcpartpicker.com/list/ or
https://buildmypc.net/ that will check your compatibility to make sure your parts will not only fit physically together but will also perform
and be compatible with each other (not create lag or bottlenecking). The biggest piece of advice that I can offer build a pc that fits YOUR needs and do
your research. There are plenty of resources on the internet that will provide valuable information and it may take several hours or even days to
to figure what's best for your wants/needs based on your budget. Now with that being said LET'S GET BUILDING!!!!!!!!!!! :D
